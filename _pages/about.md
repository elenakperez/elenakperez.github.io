I am a 5th year Physical Oceanography PhD student in the [MIT-WHOI Joint Program](https://mit.whoi.edu/) advised by [Magdalena Andres](https://www.whoi.edu/profile/mandres/) and [Glen Gawarkiewicz](https://www.whoi.edu/science/PO/people/ggg/). I passed my PhD qualifying exams in June 2023 and published my first chapter as a paper in JGR Oceans, entitled ["Is the Regime Shift in Gulf Stream Warm Core Rings Detected by Satellite Altimetry? An Inter-comparison of Eddy Identification and Tracking Products."](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1029/2023JC020761) I presented my thesis proposal to my thesis committee in April 2024 and began working on my chapter 2 after that. In winter 2025, I wrapped up my Chapter 2, which focused on understanding the drivers of the Gulf Stream variability: both its mean latitudinal position 75-70W and its stability east of the node at ~70-68W. I'm now working on Chapter 3, which will be an in-depth characterization of the Slope Gyre and Slope Gyre using a mix of satellite altimetry and sub-surface observations, primarily collected by the [CMV Oleander](https://currents.soest.hawaii.edu/oleander/flastik/index.html).

Some of my past research projects include: [understanding drivers of marine heatwaves in the North Atlantic](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-97012-0), [analyzing variability of Gulf Stream rings using sea surface height products](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1029/2023JC020761), and modeling the relationship of the Shelf Break Jet to sea level variability on the U.S. NE coast. I am particularly interested in climate variability, mesoscale to large-scale ocean dynamics, sea level change, and extreme events. 

In my free time, I enjoy biking around Cape Cod and Boston, walking in nature, swimming, listening to podcasts, and reading. During the summer I do pottery and in the winter I (attempt to work on my) knit(ting projects).
